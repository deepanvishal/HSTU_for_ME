Slide 1 — The Question & Framework

Objective (headline, top)
Can a member's first claim predict their next provider or specialty?

Visit
1 member + 1 calendar day = 1 visit. Multiple claims on the same date — PCP, Lab, Imaging — collapsed into one visit to preserve chronological sequence and eliminate billing noise.
First Encounter
Earliest date a member presents with a specific ICD-10 code. One per member per diagnosis, ever. Excludes recurrences — a returning diagnosis represents continuation of established care, not a new routing decision.
Prediction Target
Top 5 specialty categories ranked by probability. Not a specific provider. Not a visit count. Specialty type only.
Pure Sequence Input
Strictly the 20 most recent historical specialty visits before the trigger. No demographics, no geography, no provider history. Clinical trajectory alone.
Why Not Single Next Visit?
After a new diagnosis, a member rarely sees just one specialty. In 30 days they may see Primary Care, Cardiology, and Lab. In 180 days the picture shifts further. Predicting one next visit collapses all of that into a single point — losing both volume and timing signal.
Three Independent Prediction Windows

T0-30: Immediate follow-up — urgent referrals, diagnostics
T30-60: Secondary follow-up — specialist confirmations
T60-180: Chronic management — ongoing care pathway

Slide 2 — Model Results

Two Models Tested

Analytics Baseline: Markov transition model — frequency of specialty transitions from training data. No temporal awareness. Same top-5 prediction regardless of window.
Machine Learning: SASRec and BERT4Rec — sequence models that learn from visit order. Temporal awareness built in. Prediction changes per window.

Core Metric — NDCG@5
Position-aware ranking. Placing the right specialty at Rank 1 scores higher than placing it at Rank 5. Binary accuracy ignores this — NDCG captures it.
