Boundary Rules — Slide Bullets

A trigger = first time a member presents with a specific ICD-10 code. One per member per diagnosis, ever.
Recurrences (same diagnosis reappearing after 12+ months) are excluded — they represent return to an established care relationship, not a new routing decision.
Left boundary: Member must be enrolled for 12 full months before the trigger date. Ensures a clean lookback window to confirm the diagnosis is genuinely new to the data.
Right boundary: At least one full follow-up window (T30, T60, or T180) must fit within both the dataset end (Dec 2025) and the member's enrollment end. Partial windows are fully invalid — a trigger either qualifies for a window or does not.
Members who trigger near the end of the dataset or disenroll shortly after are excluded. No partial credit.


Boundary Impact Analysis — Slide Bullets

Qualification is a multi-stage funnel: raw first encounters → left qualified → window qualified → model ready.
Left boundary (12M enrollment rule) is the primary volume filter — removes triggers from members with insufficient claims history.
Right boundary losses split between two causes: dataset end truncation and member disenrollment. Both are quantified separately.
T30 retains the most triggers; T180 loses the most — longer the window, fewer triggers survive both enrollment and dataset constraints.
Triggers with no claims in the 12 months prior are valid for EDA and Markov but excluded from sequence model training — the model requires a non-empty input sequence.
Final model-ready population = T180 qualified + has prior claims. This is the most conservative and most reliable subset.
